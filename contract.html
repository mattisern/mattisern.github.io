This Participant Agreement (“Agreement”) is entered into between Mattis Erngren (“Facilitator”) and the undersigned participant (“Participant”). By enrolling in Your Energetic Arts (the “Course”), Participant agrees to the terms below.

Quick highlights (not a substitute for the full agreement)

• Fee: 900 USD for the 12-week course, paid via Stripe.

• Privacy: what’s shared in the group stays in the group.

• Recording: participants may not record. Facilitator does not provide session recordings. Written notes are provided.

• Refunds: full refund available up to 14 days before the first group session. After the course begins, refunds are generally not provided (with a safety exception).

• Safety: this is not therapy or medical care. If safety concerns arise, participation may be paused or ended.

• Liability: Facilitator’s liability is limited (see § 9).

1. Purpose and Scope

  • The Course is an educational and spiritual study circle exploring practical and philosophical approaches to esoteric and energetic arts (e.g., metaphysics, myth, energy practices, ritual, alchemy, reflection).
  • The Facilitator’s role is to provide structure, readings, exercises, and group guidance as a fellow practitioner and educator.
  • The Course is not therapy, medical treatment, psychiatric care, crisis support, or a substitute for professional services. No specific outcomes are promised or guaranteed.

2. Course Format, Dates, and Logistics

  • Duration: 12 weeks, Spring 2026 (early March through end of May).
  • Sessions: one group session per week (online, unless otherwise stated in writing).
  • Support: a private group chat for reflections and practical support.
  • Materials: curated readings and shared notes gathered into a “Book of Keys.”
  • Time zone: Central European Time (CET/CEST).
  • Communication: email for administration; typical response time within 48 hours on weekdays.

3. Fees, Payment, and Enrollment

3.1 Fee
The course fee is 900 USD per participant for the full 12 weeks.

3.2 Payment and confirmation
Payment is made via Stripe Checkout using the payment link provided on the course page (or sent by the Facilitator). Enrollment is confirmed once payment is successfully completed. Unless otherwise agreed in writing, participants should enroll no later than February 15, 2026 (or until the course is full).

3.3 What the fee covers
The 900 USD fee covers participation in the 12 weekly group sessions, access to the group chat, and access to course materials and shared notes. It also includes one (1) optional 1-on-1 check-in as described in § 4.

4. 1-on-1 Sessions (What “when needed” means)

4.1 Included session
Each Participant may book one (1) 1-on-1 session during the course at no additional cost:

  • Length: 30 minutes
  • Format: online call
  • Purpose: practical support in applying course material (clarity, structure, integration, next steps)

4.2 Additional sessions (optional, extra cost)
Additional 1-on-1 sessions may be booked for an extra fee, subject to availability:

  • 60 minutes: 150 USD
  • Availability: limited capacity (typically up to 4 total extra slots per week across all participants)
  • Booking: first-come, first-served via a scheduling link or email coordination

4.3 What “when needed” means in practice
“When needed” means: if you feel stuck, emotionally overloaded by the practices, confused about material, or want help structuring your practice, you can request a 1-on-1. The Facilitator may also suggest a 1-on-1 if it seems helpful for integration or safety. 1-on-1 sessions are supportive and educational in nature and are not therapy.

5. Missed Sessions and Materials

5.1 Attendance
Participants are encouraged to attend live sessions. However, life happens.

5.2 Recordings
To protect privacy and encourage openness, the Facilitator does not provide audio/video recordings of group sessions. Participants may not record, screen-capture, or redistribute sessions or materials without written consent from the Facilitator (see § 7).

5.3 Notes
If you miss a session, you will have access to the written session notes and any assigned practices/readings. Notes are typically shared within 72 hours after the session (often sooner).

6. Health, Mental Health, and Safety Boundaries

6.1 Not medical or psychological care
The Course is for educational and spiritual exploration. It is not medical care, psychotherapy, psychiatric treatment, or crisis support. Participants remain responsible for their own physical and mental health decisions.

6.2 Participant responsibility and suitability
The Participant affirms they are currently able to participate in a group learning environment. If you have a history of severe mental health episodes (including mania, psychosis, or severe dissociation), or you are currently in an unstable period, it may not be appropriate to participate without clinical support. If suitability is uncertain, the Facilitator may request confirmation from a licensed professional that participation is appropriate.

6.3 Safeguards during the course
The Facilitator may recommend grounding adjustments (e.g., reducing practice intensity, focusing on basics, taking a break) if a participant appears destabilized. The Facilitator may also pause or end a participant’s involvement if continuing seems likely to be harmful to the participant or the group.

6.4 Crisis and escalation policy
If you are experiencing a mental health crisis, suicidal thoughts, mania/psychosis symptoms, or feel unable to stay safe:

  • Contact emergency services or your local crisis line immediately.
  • Contact a licensed professional (therapist/doctor/psychiatric services) for clinical support.
  • The Facilitator is not equipped to provide emergency care and may be unavailable in urgent situations.

If the Facilitator becomes aware of an immediate risk of serious harm and has sufficient information to act, the Facilitator may contact emergency services. Otherwise, the Facilitator’s primary response will be to encourage appropriate professional support.

7. Confidentiality, Recording, and Group Conduct

7.1 Confidentiality
Personal sharing within the group is confidential. Participants agree not to share other participants’ personal stories, identities, chat messages, or private details outside the group without explicit permission. This does not restrict anyone from sharing their own experience in general terms.

7.2 Recording and redistribution
Participants may not record, screen-capture, transcribe, or redistribute any portion of sessions, group chat content, or materials without written consent from the Facilitator. This includes sharing materials with non-participants.

7.3 Respectful conduct
Participants must maintain respect, goodwill, and non-harassment. Disruptive, abusive, coercive, or harmful behavior is not permitted. The Facilitator may remove a participant to protect the group (see § 8.4).

8. Refunds, Removal, and Cancellation

8.1 Participant-initiated refund
A full refund is available up to 14 days before the first group session. After that point, refunds are generally not provided.

8.2 After the course begins
Once the first group session has taken place, the course is considered started and refunds are not provided, except as described in § 8.4 (safety-based removal).

8.3 Missed sessions
Missed sessions are not refundable. Notes are provided per § 5.3.

8.4 Removal for safety or conduct
The Facilitator may pause or remove a participant if participation appears harmful to the participant or the group, or if the participant violates § 7. If removal is primarily for participant safety (not misconduct), the Facilitator will issue a prorated refund for the remaining unused sessions. If removal is due to misconduct under § 7.3, a refund may be denied.

9. Liability and Assumption of Risk

9.1 Assumption of risk
Participant acknowledges that spiritual/energetic practices can affect emotions, beliefs, and subjective experience. Participant agrees to engage at their own pace, take breaks as needed, and seek professional support when appropriate.

9.2 No guarantees
The Facilitator makes no guarantees regarding outcomes, health improvements, emotional states, creative results, or life changes.

9.3 Limitation of liability
To the maximum extent permitted by law, the Facilitator will not be liable for indirect, incidental, special, consequential, or punitive damages. The Facilitator’s total liability for any claim related to the Course will not exceed the amount the Participant paid for the Course (900 USD).

10. Intellectual Property

All written, audio, and visual materials created by the Facilitator remain the property of Mattis Erngren. Participants receive a limited, non-transferable license for personal use only during and after the Course. Redistribution is not permitted without written consent.

Personal data are handled in compliance with the EU GDPR.
